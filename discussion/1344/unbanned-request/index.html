Lets do a little grammar, since you seemed to be able to rapidly improve your spelling, maybe this will take (unlike the abortion?!?!)

    I did (past)
    I am (present)
    I will (future)

    I didn't (past)
    I won't (present)
    I won't (future)
    Negated form of the same tenses. Notice that present and future are the same.

    Now, the conditional is a little confusing, because there is no past or present conditional form. This however makes sense, because the past is already known and not subject to conditional behavior. And the present is constantly being revealed. In other words, the past already happened so you can't make statements about what you might or might not do in the past. Its an interesting reflection on the structure of our thinking though, these gaps in the grammatical constructions. There is a phrase "I would have" which is a different construction and not within the scope of this lesson.

    I _____ (past)
    I _____(present)
    I would (future)

    Negations:
    I ______ (past)
    I ______ (present)
    I wouldn't (future)

    Authentic example below:

    I wouldn't give my steamid or password to those jerks again!
    or
    I wouldn't install that program that got me VAC banned again!

    In both cases these are future conditional negations. I think you've been confusing the future conditional with the present.
