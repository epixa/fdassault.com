    Haven't played WoW in 2.5 years. It's a daily battle. Friends don't let friends play WoW.

    As far as GW2, it was entertaining for about 4 weeks. Leveling up was actually a very fun experience as it encompasses all other peoples doing the same quest. The environment was quite remarkable. There isn't much end game content. You save up currency that is obtained from doing the same quest over and over again to buy gear. The limited dungeons that do exist don't drop gear, they drop more currency. It's gets to be a big grind. PvP was above average and was pretty much all I did at high levels. Each class is pretty unique. Tons of skills again which you obtain with... currency. The game had potential, but really fell short for me.

    As redbone said, Skyrim is worth at least 1 play through on any console as it is one of the better games to come out in several years.

    If you get on steam right now their doing their winter sales which usually puts recenlty released games on as much a 50% off. (Borderland2, Dishonored.) They also have weekly sales with some great games available.

    Personally, I've been hooked on the ARMA:2 mod DayZ. Ultimate zombie survival. You start with nothing, and when you die, you start again, with nothing. However, survive long enough and you can find AS-50, M107 and reek terror on other players. No game has ever been as much of an experience as DayZ has been for me.
