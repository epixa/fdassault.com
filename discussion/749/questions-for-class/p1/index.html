  • KevinWorkman June 2008 Permalink
    1. What do you think of when you hear the word "technology?"
    Anything man-made that allows him to do something that he can not do by himself.

    2. Do you consider technology good or bad?
    Technology is not inherently good or bad. A hammer is not good or bad. Building a library with the hammer, good. Hitting people with the hammer, bad. Technology has the potential to be either, it's up to us to decide how we use it.

    3. Were things better when you were a kid?
    Depends on what things you're talking about. Without you defining "things" then I'm going to say that things were not better when I was a kid.

    4. Will technology make the future better or worse?
    Better

    5. What should be our greatest concern for technology's impact upon the future?
    Not realizing its full potential due to human laziness and ignorance.

    6. What is your prediction for future technolgy?
    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technological_singularity

    7. Do you think people understand technology? If not, why not?
    Not most. Ray Kurzweil does. Technology as a whole is a multi-headed beast that not many people can fully grasp. A person can know technology, but not Technology.

    8. What technology do you find the most important?
    See number 6.

    9. What technology do you find the most troubling?
    What I find most troubling about technology is how people fail to realize the full potential it possesses. Take the internet for example. It is an almost unlimited supply of information, connections to people from different cultures, and an opportunity to express oneself in countless ways. People from just a couple decades ago would have called what we have magic, not technology. And what does the average user do with it? Stares at pictures of his friends on myspace and watches pointless youtube videos all day. What a waste.

    What is this paper for?
