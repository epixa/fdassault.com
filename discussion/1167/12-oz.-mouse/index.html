  • Nunes January 2009 Permalink
    You probably don't realize that it's not just a random show, but is a very intricate serialized story. And that everything everyone says that seems completely random, is actually super important.

    For example,
    "Heh, you know word is, and I'm not saying that this is what I think, know, or have heard to be true, seriously, but Liquor said that Mouse is up to his cans. And not only that, but New Guy. Heard he got out, right? Like someone got him out. And he's got a thing for squirrels. I'm not talking as though he's cheesy green up on 'em. I'm saying more like... Ecuadorian sleep, if you get the picture. Oh, and another thing. Roostre, you know Roostre? Up on the farm guy? All right. Well Liquor also says that a letter accidentally got delivered to him, but before he could open it - BOOM! - just like that, clock shows up. Next thing he remembers, it's day 97."

    Is actually a very informative block of script. You later get to hear that letter read aloud, the clock thing is explained in detail and a number of other vague things referenced there are important as well.

    The show isn't bad, just poorly drawn. It's written and executed incredibly well.
