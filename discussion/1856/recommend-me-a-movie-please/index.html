    Which version of Dune (David Lynch or Sci-Fi Channel?) and Kingdom of Heaven (the Director's Cut is superior) do you have? Is that the full cut of Metropolis (and if so where I can I find it... legally of course. PM me).

    As for some suggestions (alphabetized):


    12 Monkeys - mystery with a sci-fi twist by Terry Gilliam starring Bruce Willis. Involves time travel and a very good performance by Brad Pitt in the nut house.

    American Beauty - Kevin Spacey hates his life in the sub-urbs and mixes things up. He does everything that any pencil pusher with a cushy office job has ever wanted to do. It deserved the awards it won.

    From Dusk till Dawn - Just watch it. Don't look up anything about it. The experience will be great. It is a Robert Rodriguez movie (and therefore Tarantino is involved one way or another, he plays a lead role). Tarantino broke George Clooney out of prison and now they are escaping to Mexico. They take hostages and hide in a titty bar.

    Labyrinth - Seriously? You should already have this movie. MUPPETS and DAVID BOWIE!

    Let the Right One In - Not in English, but the best Vampire movie in years. It is based on a book that is on my to read list.

    Role Models - The cast doesn't look like it would be a good movie, but it is a very fun movie. It contains a giant LARP battle and the best name for a fake kingdom ever.

    Speed Racer - The movie is visually impressive. There is a lot of color in the movie and the racing is fun. It isn't a masterpiece by any stretch of the imagination. If you watched the cartoons when you were young like I did, you'll see a lot of the goofy throwbacks and references that are very nostalgic.

    Star Trek: Wrath of Khan - Amazing movie. Definitely the best Star Trek movie.

    Sunshine - Sci-fi turned slasher. It is hit or miss, I think you'd like it.

    Thank You for Smoking - I don't know why this movie isn't more popular. The cast is mainly underrated actors (Aaron Eckhart, William H. Macy, Rob Lowe) and then Katie Holmes, Adam Brody, and Robert Duvall. The humor is dry and well delivered. Great flick.

    The Producers - Your Mel Brooks collection is lacking Spring Time for Hitler.
